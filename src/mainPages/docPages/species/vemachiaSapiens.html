Summary

Vemachia sapiens ("smart wasp warrior"), V. sapiens, or Vemas are a species of humanoid wasps who share similar DNA to the warrior wasps found on Earth.

Physiology

Characteristics

Anatomy

Head

Vemas have 2 black antennae that they use to sense pheremones, balance, navigation, and temperature. They do not have a sense of smell, and utilize their antennae as a substitute.
In the daylight, Vemas can see between 300-480 nm of light, but at night, they can see between 600-800 nm. This is due to a liquid compound located in their eyes that contains proteins which bond with their cone cells. When cooled, this compound solidifies, keeping the proteins from bonding and shifting their vision.
Vemas have a proboscis to eat and speak. This is normally retracted, but muscles in the back of the mouth tighten to act like a tongue that can stick out, and right in front of those muscles are another set of muscles for shaping the proboscis when speaking. They also have mandibles that they can use to break down foods and create percussive sounds.


Arms

Vemas have 2 longer upper arms and 2 shorter lower arms, all of which have 4 joints for moving the whole arm, the rear arm, the forearm, and the wrist. They also have pincers for hands that they can use for limited grabbing, but it's more likely that they'll use multiple arms to grab instead.
