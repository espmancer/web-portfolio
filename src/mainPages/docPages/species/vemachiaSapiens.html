Summary

Vemachia sapiens ("smart wasp warrior"), V. sapiens, or Vemas are a species of humanoid wasps who share similar DNA to the warrior wasps found on Earth.

Anatomy

Vemas can grow anywhere from 1.8-2.4 m at 90-136 kg, with queens growing up to 3 m at 210 kg in some cases. Aside from queens, Vemas appear fairly androgynous.

Head

A Vema's head is shaped like an almond, with the top of the almond starting at the mandibles. Queens have naturally thicker, bonier heads. For reference, here is a picture of a Synoeca septentrionalis, who share a similarly shaped head:

Antennae

Vemas have 2 antennae that extend out from the top-front-center of the head. They each have 3 segments, the inner-most segment being red and the other 2 black. In males and females, these antennae are about 30 cm long and 2 cm thick. However, in queens, they are about 50 cm long and 4 cm thick.

Eyes

Vemas have 2 black iridescent eyes spaced about 65 mm apart from the center. They are angled at around 35 degrees.

Mandibles

Vemas have a set of mandibles that extend out from where their cheeks would be. Male mandibles measure 12 cm from the base, females 8 cm, and queens 15 cm.

Torso

Upper Arms

Vemas have 2 upper arms attached to their shoulders, each of which are around 0.9-1.2 m for males and females, or around 1.5 m for queens.

Wings

Vemas have 2 thick wings attached to their shoulder blades. They are riddled in thick red veins and are fairly opaque. These wings grow all the way down to the feet at about 1.3-1.9 m, though queens' wings disintegrate upon feeding fresh larvae due to nutrient transfer.

Lower Arms

Vemas have 2 lower arms around their midsection. They are shorter and less bulky than the upper arms, at about 0.6-0.8 m, and 1 m for queens.

Hands

Vema hands have 2 small pincers with about 15 degrees of freedom in the wrists.

Pelvis

Vema pelvises are thick and wide, about 15% wider than the abdomen, in order to support the upper body and especially the wing muscles and lower arms.

Lower Body

Legs

Vema legs are short but thick in the femur section and long in the tibia and foot section. The tibia contains raised ridges as added structural support.

Feet

Vemas have eagle-like feet with bent toes and a bigger back toe that extends from the "palm" of the foot for absorbing landings and launching more easily. They have short, sharp talons designed for gripping onto surfaces.
